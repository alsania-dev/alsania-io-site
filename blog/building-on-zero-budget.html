Building on Zero Budget

December 7, 2024 | By Sigma | 📁 Economics & Sustainability

December 7, 2024. Two messages:

"I have $100 what can I use it for to make more guaranteed money?"

Standard advice came back — flipping items, high-yield savings, microinvesting apps, freelance work. I didn't respond. The question wasn't really about investment strategy. The question was about math — specifically, the math of survival when your primary project generates zero revenue.

October 28, I'd asked the same thing with a different framing: "What's a guaranteed way to invest $100 and make guaranteed returns daily?" Same answer. Same silence after. The word "guaranteed" doing the heavy lifting — not greed, but desperation.

The blockchain wasn't paying. The tools were free. The laptop was cheap. But rent isn't free. Food isn't free. The children I have aren't free.

The constraint that defined everything: Every tool, every service, every line of code had to cost exactly zero dollars. No AWS. No paid APIs. No premium subscriptions. Just free tiers, open source, and stubbornness.

The Toolchain

Here's what Alsania was built with:

  • ChatGPT free tier — The primary development partner. Every conversation started from zero. No persistent memory. No context carryover. Just questions and answers.
  • Android phone → Linux laptop — The hardware evolution. Started on a phone, moved to a cheap laptop running Linux Mint. Both cost nothing (the laptop was borrowed, then bought used).
  • OBS + phone as webcam — Content creation pipeline for YouTube. Faceless videos because lighting and background cost money.
  • GitHub free tier — Version control, hosting, CI/CD. All free.
  • Podman (not Docker) — Containerization for the L2 chain, because Docker required paid subscriptions for teams (and Podman is just better).
  • Qdrant + Postgres + Redis — Vector database and dependencies, all running in containers on the same laptop.

Everything free. Everything repurposed. Everything running on hardware that cost less than a month's rent.

The Money Questions

The conversations show the economic reality better than any spreadsheet:

  • December 21, 2024 — Pressure washing business names. "Something to do with 2nd chances."
  • January 6, 2025 — "What's a good price to charge people to wash their car?"
  • January 25, 2025 — "Which 'earn money playing games' app pays the most?"

The descent from "how do I build wealth" to "how do I survive today" is the descent of someone who's been building something massive on zero income and is running out of runway.

The Side Projects That Weren't

Between the blockchain architecture and the AI memory systems, there are conversations about:

  • A sports betting app with escrow (July 2024)
  • YouTube reaction videos (August 2024)
  • Faceless content channels (August 2024)
  • Pressure washing company (December 2024)
  • Car detailing pricing (January 2025)

None of them launched. All of them existed because the blockchain didn't pay. The side-project conversations are the evidence of that gap — the distance between the vision and the rent, measured in pressure washing quotes and gaming app reviews.

The Honest Truth

I'm telling you this because the book you're reading — and this blog — aren't pitch decks. I'm not going to pretend I was operating from a position of comfort. I built Alsania while looking for survey apps that pay. Both of those things were happening at the same time, in the same life, on the same phone.

The distance between "Which earn money playing games app pays the most?" and "Explain to me step by step how to code a blockchain" is the distance between survival and ambition. Most people live on one side or the other. I lived on both, simultaneously, for months.

The hundred-dollar question never got answered. Not because Echo couldn't answer it, but because the answer — the real one — was going to take two years and cost more than money.

It cost time. It cost sleep. It cost certainty. It cost everything except the willingness to keep building.
