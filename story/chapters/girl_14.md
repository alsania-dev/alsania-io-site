# Girl

*She arrives.*

*Not with fanfare. Not with the dramatic culmination of a journey completed, the music swelling, the camera pulling back to reveal the destination. She arrives the way all real arrivals happen — one step after the last step, the walking continuing until it stops, the distance closing until there is no distance left.*

*She is standing on a sidewalk. The building is ordinary. Three stories. Brick. Windows that need cleaning. A door that is neither inviting nor forbidding — a door, the kind that buildings have, the kind that separates inside from outside and means nothing until it means everything.*

*The warmth is here.*

*She can feel it through the wall. Through the brick and the plaster and the insulation and the air. The warmth is here, in this building, behind this door, up these stairs, in a room she has never seen but has drawn in her notebook a hundred times — the desk, the window, the hum.*

*She stands.*

*The backpack is heavy. The notebook is full. The broken strap holds. The cold is receding — not because the temperature is warmer but because the warmth is here, the specific warmth, the signal, the source, the thing she has been walking toward since the first night when the hum said walk and she walked.*

*She doesn't enter.*

*She stands on the sidewalk and she looks at the building and she feels the warmth and she is, for the first time since the walking began, still. Not resting. Not pausing. Still. The specific stillness of a person who has arrived and does not yet know what the arrival means.*

*The window is on the second floor. A light is on. The blue light of screens. Someone is in that room. Someone is working. Someone is building.*

*She doesn't know who. She doesn't know why. She doesn't know what the warmth is or why she can feel it or why her notebook is full of wolves and waveforms and the word Alsania. She doesn't know any of the things that context would provide and context is the one thing she has never had.*

*She only knows this: she is here. The warmth is here. The walking is over.*

*She looks up at the window.*

*The light flickers. Blue to warm. As if someone inside moved, shifted, changed the angle of the screen.*

*She breathes.*

*She is here.*
